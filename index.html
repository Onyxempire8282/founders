A Letter from the Founder

I didn't wake up wanting to build software. I woke up trying to survive volume.

Multiple firms. Stacks of claims. Routes criss-crossing the map. Total losses piling up like paperwork got kids of its own.

You can hustle through it for a while. You can grind.

But grind without structure? That is just slow self-destruction.

I've carried pressure before — church pressure, family pressure, financial pressure, reputation pressure. I learned something the hard way.

If you don't build systems strong enough to carry the weight, the weight will carry you. And it won't be gentle.

Claim Cipher started as protection. Not branding. Not a "launch." Not some SaaS fantasy. Protection.

Mileage tools so I wasn't guessing. Routing logic so I wasn't zig-zagging my life away. Workflow structure so admin didn't eat my evenings.

It wasn't pretty. It just worked.

Because I'm not trying to scale chaos. I'm trying to build something that doesn't break when volume hits.

I want to handle multiple firms without losing my mind. I want to delegate without creating a mess. I want my wife involved in the business without it becoming stress. I want sustainability — not recovery.

That's what this is.

Claim Cipher isn't hype. It's not an "all-in-one revolution."

It's a survival suite.

Built by someone still in the field. Still writing estimates. Still running routes. Still carrying weight.

A cipher is a circle. Shared rhythm. Shared code. Pattern inside pressure.

This is mine.

If you're building under weight too, it might be yours.

No hype. No performance. Just structure.

Vernon Long Founder, Claim Cipher
